The Thinking

AEO is a discipline, not a dashboard

Here's my biggest pet peeve in this space. Someone hears "AEO" for the first time and their immediate reaction is: "What tool do I buy?" That's backwards. That's like hearing "brand building" and asking which survey platform to subscribe to. The tool doesn't fix the problem. The discipline fixes the problem.

AEO is a discipline — like content marketing, like brand strategy. You don't fix AI visibility by purchasing a better dashboard. You fix it by creating better content on the domain you control. The dashboard just tells you whether it's working. And if you haven't done the hard work of content quality, trust signals, and structured authority — the dashboard is going to tell you the same bad news every month.

Here's the reframing that changes everything: your first sales call now happens inside an AI answer. Think about what's actually happening when a buyer opens ChatGPT and spends 15–20 minutes getting personalized recommendations, comparing options, asking follow-up questions about pricing and capabilities. That's a sales conversation. It's just happening without you in the room. If you're not in the answer, you've lost a deal you never even knew existed.

That's not hypothetical. We're seeing AI traffic convert at 3–5x the rate of organic search. Ramp gets cited consistently in AI answers about expense management — and they didn't get there by buying a monitoring tool. They got there by being genuinely useful on their own site.

So how do you actually build the discipline? We use the PRAI framework — four dimensions that give you a complete picture of your AI visibility:

→ Presence — Are you even showing up? Not once, not sometimes — consistently, across engines. ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini. Each one behaves differently. If you're visible in one and invisible in three, you have a presence problem.
→ Reputation — When you do show up, how are you described? Is the language accurate? Is it favorable? Or is AI surfacing three-year-old complaints from a review site you forgot about?
→ Alignment — Does what AI says about you match what you actually want to be known for? This is your biggest lever. The gap between your positioning and AI's perception of you — that's where the work lives.
→ Influence — Can you systematically improve all three over time? This isn't a one-time audit. This is an ongoing practice. The companies that win here are the ones that treat it like a muscle, not a project.

The sphere of influence model makes this actionable. Start at the center — your website. It's fully controlled, it compounds over time, and it's where transactions happen. Then work outward to platforms you influence — LinkedIn, YouTube, GitHub, review sites. Things where you can publish and engage but don't own the algorithm. Then the outer ring: things you earn. Analyst reports, press coverage, word of mouth. Starting at the center isn't about ignoring the outer rings. It's about building from what you control so the outer rings have something real to reference.

When we ran the Webflow workshop, Guy Yalif laid out the maturity model that makes this concrete. Four dimensions, each with a progression:

→ Content maturity: keywords → answers. Stop optimizing for keyword strings. Start creating content that directly answers the questions buyers are asking.
→ Technical maturity: on-page SEO → structured schema. AI engines need machine-readable signals — not just good copy.
→ Authority maturity: backlinks → mentions. Links still matter, but AI citation runs on mentions, context, and trust signals that go beyond PageRank.
→ Measurement maturity: ranking → share-of-voice. Position 1 on Google is one metric. Share-of-voice across AI engines is the metric that actually tells you whether you're winning.

Most companies are at level one in most of these dimensions. They don't realize it because they're measuring the old way — rankings, traffic, bounce rates. The maturity model shows you where you actually stand and what the next level looks like.

And if you want proof this works — Webflow went from 0% to 42% LLM traffic in one year. A 6x conversion rate compared to traditional organic. This isn't theory. This is first-party data from a company that took the discipline seriously, invested in content quality, fixed their technical foundation, and measured what actually mattered.

The companies winning in AI visibility aren't buying better tools. They're thinking better about content, quality, and trust. That's the discipline — and it starts with what you control.

Key Takeaways

What sticks

  • AEO is a discipline, not a tool. You don't fix AI visibility by buying a dashboard — you fix it by creating better content.
  • The 4-pillar maturity model (Content, Technical, Authority, Measurement) diagnoses where you're falling short.
  • PRAI (Presence, Reputation, Alignment, Influence) is the operational framework for AI visibility.
  • Webflow went from 0% to 42% LLM traffic in one year with 6x conversion — proof the model works.
  • Your sphere of influence: control (your site) → influence (LinkedIn, YouTube) → earn (analyst reports, press).
